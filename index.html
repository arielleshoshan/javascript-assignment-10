Powerlifting: The Three Main Compound Movements

By: Arielle Shoshan

In this assignment, I used JavaScript to manipulate a few of these images. I also explain the three different main compound movements in Powerlifting. There is a fun interactive quiz at the end!

Squat

The squat is the first compound movement where you stand shoulder width apart with toes pointed outward propgating a proper pathway for your knees to hinge. You begin this movement standing, lowering your body while keeping your back straight, and your chin tucked. Hinge at the knees to get the full range of motion.

Bench

The bench press is the second compound movement where you lie on the bench, arch your back slightly, and press the barbell off your chest in an upward motion. Keeping your shoulder blades retracted and your elbows tucked in, then press up and fully extend your arms to get the full range of motion.

Deadlift

The deadlift is the third compound movement where you begin in a standing position either in sumo or conventional stance. You pull the barbell to your shins and straighten your back. Pull the slack out of the bar, then pull up the bar in a straight bar path till you're standing and fully lock out the position to achieve the full range of motion.
